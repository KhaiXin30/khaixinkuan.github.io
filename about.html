I'm Khai

[This is my honest attempt to introduce who I am beyond titles (,,>﹏<,,)]

I’m someone who knows what I don’t want, and I’m patiently exploring what I want most in life. I refuse to copy the definition of success around me, especially at an Ivy League. I’m an explorer— the kind of person who only commits to something after I’ve tried it, experienced it, and felt it for myself.

I grew up not dreaming about FAANG, MBB, bulge bracket banks, or Silicon Valley. I didn’t fully understand that world until my first month at Cornell. Even then, I’ve never been able to give a simple “yes” or “no,” because I can see both the beauty and the ugliness on each side. So I kept experimenting— joining clubs, entering competitions, talking to others, and listening closely for my own voice beneath all the noise.

That same adventurous spirit follows me beyond campus. Almost every Cornell break is spent somewhere new — new places, new faces, new cultures, new food. I enjoy stepping into the unknown. I like the surprises. I like the adrenaline.

Another thing about me is that I love listening more than talking. I’m drawn to people with perspectives different from my own, what I call “different energy.” One coincidental hackathon led me into the world of crypto and Bitcoin, where I met people with entirely different beliefs about money and the financial system. As always, I didn’t rush to judge. I chose to listen. I became a researcher for a Bitcoin study at Cornell and interviewed people around the world to understand what Bitcoin and crypto meant to them— not as headlines, but as something personal.

And I know how lucky I am: to be here, to learn, to receive a world-class education. It didn’t happen solely because of me but because people helped me along the way. For a long time I wondered how to give that back. Eventually, I realized I could start by doing what they did for me. So I committed my weekends to volunteering with Girls Who Code, teaching middle school students how to code. The satisfaction of watching them grow— the confidence, the curiosity, the moment when something finally clicks— is hard to describe, but can fuel you through another rough and intense week.

Right now, I’m looking for a summer internship or co-op where I can keep learning by building and thinking boldly (especially in roles that blend data, technology, and real-world business problems). If any part of my story resonates with you, I’d love to connect!

Volunteering

I’ve been volunteering with Girls Who Code for the past four semesters, growing from a volunteer to a co-leader. Every summer, I also volunteer with USAPPS, helping promote U.S. education to fellow Malaysians.

Sports

Khai without sports is incomplete. I enjoy racquet sports like table tennis, badminton, and pickleball, and I love hiking and camping. In my King Scout days, I was obsessed with backwoods cooking (sth not everyone understands unfortunately).
