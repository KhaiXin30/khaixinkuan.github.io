I’m pretty stubborn with myself. I care about being useful, getting better, and building a life that actually feels like mine.

Cornell introduced me to the “FAANG/MBB/bulge bracket” universe. Honestly, a lot of it sounded attractive. Each path had something I could see myself enjoying. That also made me feel lost, and for a while I couldn’t bring myself to commit. I don’t want my decisions to be driven by noise, so I tried things, reflected, and waited for something to feel truly worth going deep on.

My biggest lesson, though, is that not choosing can be risky. There’s a fine line between searching and quietly running away when things get tough. And if I’m being real, it can be a waste of time too. Now I’m clear: I get genuinely excited about using technology to solve business problems (best if data is part of the solution).

I love new places, new cultures, and that little adrenaline rush of not knowing what’s next. I’m also the type who likes to listen more than talk. I’m drawn to people with totally different perspectives, what I call “different energy.” That’s how a random hackathon led me into Bitcoin/crypto… and somehow into a Bitcoin study at Cornell, interviewing people around the world, and understanding what Bitcoin and crypto meant to them.

I’m grateful I’m here, receiving world class education, and I try to give that back. On weekends, I volunteer with Girls Who Code and teach middle school students how to code. Watching them go from confused to confident is what fuels me through rough, intense weeks.

Right now, I’m looking for a summer internship or co-op, ideally something that mixes data, tech, and real business problems. If any of this resonates, I’d love to connect.

Volunteering

I’ve been volunteering with Girls Who Code for the past four semesters, growing from a volunteer to a co-leader. Every summer, I also volunteer with USAPPS, helping promote U.S. education to fellow Malaysians.

Sports
